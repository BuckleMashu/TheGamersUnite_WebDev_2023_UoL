As we were walking toward the bus stop opposite the Bedok mall to catch a bus, laughter can be heard. We were catching up on each other's lives, sharing hilarious things we found and did. But suddenly, right before we reach the bus stop...our stomache grumbled. We decided to eat McDonald for lunch first.

What we made

Cherry Tomato Pasta Loaded Mash Potato
Ingredients Measurement Notes
Olive Oil 3 Tablespoon(Tbsp) 1 Tbsp for pasta boiling, 2 Tbsp for the sauce
Garlic 6 cloves Its best to cut the garlic into thin slices for the aroma
Onion Half a head Chopped finely. Onions can be subsitud with 3 Shallots
Cherry Tomatoes 14 14 cherry Tomatoes are meant for 2 serving. We forgotton to buy more
Pasta 500 grams I recommend Fusilli pasta for this dish as it will hold onto the sauce the very well
Basil 3/4 cup Basil is important in this dish as it provide both great aroma and flavour when we don't use meat.
Tomato paste 3 Tbsp Suprisingly, this can actually be substituted with ketchup since we want to push the tomato flavour. (But I do not recommend this if you want to cook for your Italian friends
Seasoning and Spices: salt, sugar, mixed dried herb,etc. Add to taste You can add sugar to balance out the dish if its slightly too sour from the tomatoes

Preparation

Step 1

bring the Fusilli pasta, 2 teaspoons of salt, and 1 tablespoon of olive oil into a pot of boiling water. Cook the pasta until it is al dente as it will continue to cook in the pan later. The recommended cooking time is 10 - 15 minutes, afterward, drain them. Do remember to save some pasta water for the sauce later.


Step 2

Process the vegetables. Wash them, chop the basil into thin strip, dice the onion, and slice the garlic thinly.


Step 3

Add 2 tablespoons of olive oil, chopped garlic and onion to a medium temperature pan. Becareful of the temperature as we dont want the garlic to burn too much


Step 4

After the onion turn translucent, add the cherry tomatoes and 4 tablespoons of saved pasta water into the pan. Then close the lidfor 3 minutes, as we want to the tomatoes to soften quickly


Step 5

Smash the cherry tomatoes, add the 2 tablespoons of tomato paste, and stir for 1 minutes on medium high heat. This is so to give the sauce a slight pleasent burn flavour


Step 6

Lower the heat back to medium and add the pasta and basil. Stir for 2 minutes, cover and turn off the heat to allow the residual heat to finish the cooking

You can add any additional salt, sugar, pepper,or dried herb at Step 5 or 6 to your taste.

Ingredients Measurement Notes
Yukon Gold Potatoes 5 We picked this type of potato because it is balance, semi-starchy and semi-waxy
Smoked Bacon 200 grams We are going to make it into bacon bits
Sour Cream 123 grams Can be substituted with greek yogurt if you count your calories
Mozzarella Cheese 200 grams You can replace it with any cheese of your choice
Seasoning and Spices: salt, sugar, mixed dried herb,etc. Add to taste You can add sugar to balance out the dish if its slightly too sour or salty from the sour cream or bacon respectively

Preparation

Step 1

Fry the bacons in the pan. Cook until they are crispy and let them rest on a plate with paper towel. Afterward, crush them to bits.


Step 2

Peel the potatoes and chop them into 8 chunks each. Afterward, bring them to boil in a pot of water. Remember to add 2 teaspon of salt.


Step 3

When the potatoes are sufficicently soften for the knives/fork to slide into, strain out the water from the pot.


Step 4

While the potatoes are hot, shred the mozzarella cheese and add bacon bits into pot. Then start mashing the potatoes up.


Step 5

After the cheese is melted, add in sour cream and continue the mashing.



You can add any additional salt, sugar, pepper,or dried herb to your taste.

One day, we were sitting around trying to figure out what to do for the evening. We wanted to hang out, but we were all tired of the usual routine of going out to eat or grabbing drinks at a bar. That's when thanks to Mr. Foo Jun “Omakase Chef” Peng and Mr. Adip “Head Chef” Herman, they have blessed us with knowing what to cook for our meet-up and we all loved the idea. After arriving at Jun Peng’s house, we decide to relax in his room and entertain ourselves. Talking about how I found a mini Quran keychain on Shoppee and immediately bought it, what are the plans for our modded Minecraft server, changing Jun Peng’s desktop background picture, playing chess, etc. Just doing things that are of our interest.



Cooking is a great way to bring people together. There's something about sharing a meal that makes us feel connected and happy (definitely not us messing around). What I love about cooking together is that it's a team effort. Everyone has a role to play, whether it's chopping vegetables, stirring a pot, or setting the table. It's a great way to bond . We talked about how to process certain ingredients to our next major travel plans. It's amazing how much we enjoy one another's company when we're all in the kitchen together.

Through out our cooking process, we joked around, A LOT. Things we did can ranges from juggling potatoes, capitalising one's mistake, to Adip calling others sous chefs.



In conclusion, cooking dinner together has become a highlight of my week. It's amazing to see how much we've grown as friends by simply spending time in the kitchen. Cooking together has taught us teamwork(maybe), patience(possibly), and the value of good food(100% 🔥). I believe that sharing a meal with friends is one of life's greatest pleasures, and cooking it together is even better. So, if you're looking for a fun and rewarding way to bond with your friends, I highly recommend giving cooking together a try. You won't regret it!
