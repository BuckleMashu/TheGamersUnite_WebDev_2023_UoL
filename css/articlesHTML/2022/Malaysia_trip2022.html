Our trip to Malaysia

17th September 2022

Kuala Lumpur (KL), the capital city of Malaysia, is located in the west-central part of the country, and it is the largest city in Malaysia with a population of over 7 million people in the greater metropolitan area.

Figure 1: Preparation for the flight

Kuala Lumpur is a bustling and vibrant city, known for its skyscrapers, shopping districts, and cultural landmarks. Some of the notable attractions in Kuala Lumpur include the Petronas Twin Towers, the Batu Caves, the Islamic Arts Museum Malaysia, and the Merdeka Square. The city is also home to a diverse range of restaurants and street food vendors, serving up a variety of traditional Malay, Chinese, and Indian dishes.

As the capital city of Malaysia, Kuala Lumpur is also an important center for business, commerce, and finance. Many multinational corporations have their offices and headquarters in the city, and it is also home to several international conferences and exhibitions.

Goodbye Malaysia, for now

Figure 16: Adip(left) and Murray(right)

This trip also allows us to experience a different culture. Even though Singapore and Malaysia are quite close to one another, we can really see the differences in cultures through their cooking’s spices and slang.

For example, when I order a cup of water in Malaysia, I have to say “Ice Kosong”. “Ice” is meant “chilled/iced drink” in this context, while “Kosong” is a slang for “no sugar”. So when we order “Iced Kosong”, we are technically ordering ice cubes.

As we said our goodbyes to Kuala Lumpur, we knew that we had experienced something truly special. Our trip had brought us closer together as friends, and we left Malaysia with a deeper appreciation for its culture, its people, and its place in the world.
